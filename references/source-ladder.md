# Source Ladder

Use this ladder to decide what can count as evidence, what needs cross-checking, and what should remain only a lead.

## How to use this ladder

For every retained item, classify the supporting material into one of three levels:

1. `首选证据` — can directly support a retained claim
2. `次选证据` — can support a claim if cross-checked with stronger material
3. `仅作线索` — useful for discovery, not enough for the final assertion on its own

If a claim touches regulation, capital markets, safety, scientific validity, or geopolitics, prefer at least one `首选证据`.

## Universal ladder

### 首选证据

1. Official statements, filings, and primary documents
2. Regulator, ministry, exchange, court, standards body, or institution releases
3. Original direct reporting from a reputable outlet
4. Company earnings materials and investor presentations when used with caution and context

### 次选证据

1. Reputable secondary reporting that cites named sources or primary materials
2. Established industry databases and research summaries
3. Well-sourced trade media
4. Curated community or platform signals when clearly separated from factual proof

### 仅作线索

1. Unsourced aggregation
2. Social media posts without corroboration
3. Opinion pieces presented as if they were factual reporting
4. Viral reposts and screenshot-only claims
5. Hotlist rankings or engagement counts without underlying reporting

## Politics and policy

### 首选证据

1. Government websites, ministries, white papers, official briefings
2. Treaty text, bill text, court decisions, gazettes
3. Named direct reporting from credible outlets on policy or diplomacy

### 次选证据

1. Think tank summaries
2. Policy newsletters with citations
3. Reputable explainers that link back to official text

### 仅作线索

1. Anonymous-policy-thread social posts
2. Clip-only interpretations without the full speech or document
3. Rumor accounts claiming impending policy shifts

## International hotspots and geopolitics

### 首选证据

1. Official statements from governments and international organizations
2. Satellite, shipping, or conflict-monitoring data when from credible sources
3. Direct reporting from reputable international correspondents

### 次选证据

1. Reputable regional press summaries
2. Analyst notes that clearly separate fact from interpretation

### 仅作线索

1. War-map reposts without attribution
2. Anonymous Telegram or X claims
3. Single-source battlefield rumors

## Business and capital markets

### 首选证据

1. Exchange filings, regulator releases, earnings calls, company disclosures
2. Official company statements and investor relations materials
3. Original reporting on deals, earnings, or management changes from credible financial outlets

### 次选证据

1. Reputable market data terminals or summaries
2. Broker notes or analyst writeups when clearly labeled as analysis
3. Trade press with named sourcing

### 仅作线索

1. Social posts about “breaking” deals without filings
2. Chatroom rumor, stock-forum speculation, screenshot-only claims
3. Anonymous “people familiar” summaries copied across low-quality sites

## Mergers, acquisitions, and primary market

### 首选证据

1. Signed deal announcements, filings, antitrust notices
2. Official fund announcements, LP letters when legitimately accessible
3. Direct reporting from credible finance outlets naming the deal context

### 次选证据

1. Venture databases
2. Fundraising trackers
3. Sector newsletters with attribution

### 仅作线索

1. “融资将近完成” style rumor posts
2. Unverified fundraising screenshots
3. Deal chatter without named parties or filings

## AI, embodied intelligence, and frontier tech

### 首选证据

1. Official model, product, company, or lab release
2. Technical report, benchmark appendix, or demo with inspectable details
3. Direct reporting that cites named executives, labs, or released artifacts

### 次选证据

1. Research newsletters with source links
2. Developer analysis that reproduces the result
3. Trade press summaries
4. Hacker News, Reddit, GitHub release notes, or practitioner discussions used as context rather than sole proof

### 仅作线索

1. Hype threads without artifacts
2. Demo clips with no supporting documentation
3. Capability claims copied from marketing language alone

## Academic research

### 首选证据

1. Published paper, preprint, proceedings page, or official conference record
2. Journal website, DOI-linked page, Crossref metadata
3. Official institutional release that links back to the underlying work

### 次选证据

1. University newsroom summaries
2. Scholarly newsletters
3. Science media summaries that clearly cite the paper

### 仅作线索

1. “New study says” articles with no citation
2. Abstract screenshots without source page
3. Social summaries by non-authors with no links

## Entertainment, film, and TV

### 首选证据

1. Studio, platform, distributor, festival, or box office official release
2. Trades with direct reporting and named sourcing
3. Official release calendar, ratings announcement, or platform slate

### 次选证据

1. Major entertainment press summaries
2. Interview-based recaps that clearly cite source material

### 仅作线索

1. Fan rumor accounts
2. Anonymous spoiler posts
3. Screenshot-only casting or renewal rumors

## Sports

### 首选证据

1. League, club, federation, tournament, or athlete official announcement
2. Verified competition results pages
3. Direct reporting from reputable sports desks

### 次选证据

1. Beat reporter aggregation
2. Broadcaster recaps that cite official results

### 仅作线索

1. Transfer rumor farms
2. Injury speculation without team confirmation
3. Viral clips detached from context

## Energy storage, charging, power electronics, and energy systems

### 首选证据

1. Regulator and standards body releases
2. Official project announcements, tender notices, safety notices, technical specifications
3. Direct reporting on deployments, incidents, or policy from credible sector outlets

### 次选证据

1. Company presentations with cross-checking
2. Industry associations, market summaries, and research houses
3. Trade press with named sources
4. Engineering community discussion, open-source protocol repositories, or conference-side signals used only as context

### 仅作线索

1. Vendor marketing claims without project evidence
2. Viral engineering claims with no datasheet, standard, or deployment reference
3. Screenshots of slides with no source deck

## Community and hotlist signals

Treat these as context, attention, or early discovery tools unless they link back to stronger evidence.

### 次选证据

1. Platform-native release notes, repo release pages, or maintainer posts with inspectable artifacts
2. High-signal community threads that clearly link to the underlying project, paper, filing, or product page

### 仅作线索

1. Hot-search boards and trending lists by themselves
2. Upvote counts, likes, repost counts, or comment counts without source follow-up
3. Community excitement detached from the underlying document or release

## Culture and society

### 首选证据

1. Official agency releases, survey data, court records, institutional reports
2. Direct reporting from credible outlets

### 次选证据

1. NGO or think tank summaries
2. Well-sourced magazine features

### 仅作线索

1. Social virality alone
2. Clip fragments detached from original setting
3. Meme-page claims

## Escalation rules

Escalate to stronger evidence when:

1. Numbers differ across sources
2. Dates or timeline are inconsistent
3. The item could move markets or policy expectations
4. The claim depends on a scientific or technical performance metric
5. The source is too close to the subject and may be promotional

## Final rule

If an item only has `仅作线索` material, either:

1. keep it out of the main digest, or
2. place it in `继续跟踪` and label it as unconfirmed
